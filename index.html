Reading Passage

The concept of artificial intelligence has evolved dramatically since its inception in the 1950s. Initially, researchers believed that creating machines capable of human-like thinking would be a relatively straightforward task, achievable within a few decades. However, as scientists delved deeper into the complexities of human cognition, they discovered that replicating intelligence artificially was far more challenging than anticipated.

Early AI systems were rule-based, relying on explicit programming to handle specific tasks. These systems excelled in narrow domains but struggled with the flexibility and adaptability that characterize human intelligence. The breakthrough came with the development of machine learning algorithms, particularly neural networks, which could learn patterns from data rather than following pre-programmed rules.
